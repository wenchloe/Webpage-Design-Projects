Background Information
Birthday: July 12th, 1997
Birthplace: Mingora, Pakistan (Swat Valley)
Parents: Tor Pekai Yousafzai and Ziauddin Yousafzai
Siblings: Atal Yousafzai and Khushal Yousafzai
Education: She attended school at her father's school in Swat Valley
Economic Background: Middle Class in Pakistan

Her father, Ziauddin Yousafzai, was her major influence and inspiration to speak out about the lack of education for women in Pakistan and other countries around the area. Ziauddin Yousafzai was an activist and speaker who was against the Taliban, a group that was shutting down schools for girls at an alarming rate with violence. His actions would soon lead Malala on a path to international fame after getting shot in the head and recovering.
BBC Blog and the Taliban
  • Around 2007-2008, the Taliban began attacking schools for girls in Swat Valley (10 years)
  • In 2009, Malala began a BBC blog about living under the Taliban. She took on the name Gul Makai to hide her identity (11 years)
  • BBC Blogger of 2009
  • After her identity was revealed in 2011, Taliban issued a death threat against her (13 years)
  • She won the 2011 Pakistan's National Youth Peace Prize, later the Malala Peace Prize
Tragedy on October 9th, 2012
  • On October 9th, 2012, a Taliban troop boarded the bus Malala was riding in and demanded to know where she was
  • Although her friends tried to hide her, the Taliban found Malala and shot her
  • The bullet hit the left side of Malala's head and traveled to her neck
  • She was flown to a hospital in Peshawar where part of her skull was removed before being transferred to Birmingham Hospital in England
  • With this tragedy came organized protests, prayers, and more. Many held vigil over a candle and united for her cause
Recovery and Backlash
  • After making a speedy recovery and avoiding major brain damage, Malala was left with lingering paralyzation int he left side of her face
  • Since her incident, Malala has gained world-wide attention and an overwhelming amount of outpour from youths all over the world
  • She currently has an autobiography out and recently won the 2014 Nobel Peace Prize
  • Malala has also been faced with backlash by Pakistan and more, with some schools banning her book. When she returned to Pakistan, she was also physically assaulted
  • Showing her tremendous grit, she still works hard to this day, launching campaigns and becoming an international symbol of hope.
Role Model
  • Malala keeps fighting to this day for the rights of her people, even though she was shot and still has a death order out for her
  • After returning home, she was outcasted and pelted with rocks
  • She has gotten much backlash, verbally and on the internet, by her own people and others elsewhere
